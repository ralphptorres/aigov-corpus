# EXPLANATORY NOTE

Inrecent years,technology-particularly social media，has profoundly transformed the dynamics of communication,information dissemination,and public discourse. This digital shift presents immense opportunities to enhance political participation and promote greater transparency in the electoral process.

Citizens now have unprecedented access to information and platforms for civic engagement, enabling more inclusive and participatory governance. Notably, these advancements have also brought new challenges and risks, such as the spread of misinformation, data manipulation,and online harassment,which threaten the integrity of democratic institutions and public trust.

Arecent study found thata majority of Filipinos struggle to detect fake news, and surveys identify the internet and social media, particularly through influencers, as the primary sources of disinformation. This is a concerning combination, given that Filipinos are among the top social media users globally.1 This reality underscore the need for comprehensive policies that leverage the benefits of digital technology while safeguarding the credibility of electoral and governance systems.

The country's primary election law, the Omnibus Election Code (Batas Pambansa Blg.881),was enacted long before the rise of social media and modern frameworks to ensure they remain responsive to the realities of the digital age. Meanwhile, the Fair Elections Act(Republic ActNo.9006)allows election propaganda through traditional media and "any other medium," subject to authorized spending limits,truth in advertising,and supervision by the Commission on Elections (COMELEC).2 This provision has served as the legal basis for COMELEC to regulate digital media.However, the absence of clear statutory definitionsand mechanisms addressing modern digital offenses has made enforcement inconsistent and prosecution difficult. These gaps emphasize the urgency of strengthening safeguards to protect electoral integrity amid the growing influence of technology on public opinion and voter behavior.

Therefore, this proposed bill seeks to establish a clear and comprehensive framework for regulating digital and online election activities to uphold transparency, fairness, and accountability throughout the electoral process. It aims to empower the Commission on Elections (COMELEC) with the necessary tools and authority to address emerging challenges in the digital sphere, such as online misinformation, disinformation, data manipulation,and the misuse of artificial intelligence in political campaigns.

By strengthening institutional mechanisms and updating existing policies, this bill envisions a more secure and credible electoral environment that fully adapts to the realities of modern communication technologies while safeguarding the integrity of the nation's democratic processes.

In view of the foregoing, the immediate passage of this bill is earnestly sought.
