# HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 115

Introduced by REP. BERNADETTE “DETTE” G. ESCUDERO

# RESOLUTION URGING THE APPROPRIATE COMMITTEE TO CONDUCT AN INQUIRY, IN AID OF LEGISLATION, ON THE RESPONSIBLE AND ETHICAL USE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE (AI) IN THE PHILIPPINE EDUCATION SYSTEM, WITH THE END IN VIEW OF CRAFTING A NATIONAL POLICY FRAMEWORK THAT PROMOTES ACADEMIC INTEGRITY, SUPPORTS HUMAN COGNITION, AND SAFEGUARDS LEARNING IN THE AGE OF GENERATIVE AI

WHEREAS, Article 2, Section 17 of the 1987 Constitution provides that "[t]he State shall give priority to education, science and technology, arts, culture and sports to foster patriotism and nationalism, accelerate social progress, and promote total human liberation and development."; 

WHEREAS, the emergence and continued evolution of Artificial Intelligence (AI), particularly generative AI technologies, such as tools such as ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot, are reshaping various aspects of society, including the country’s education system by transforming how information is accessed, processed, and presented by students, teachers, and academic institutions. These generative Ais are increasingly accessible to students across the country and are now widely used in producing academic requirements, including essays, reports, and presentations; 

WHEREAS, existing policies across schools and universities regard plagiarism as a grave academic offense, warranting penalties such as suspension or expulsion. However, there remains no legislation or policy determining whether, or under what circumstances, the use of AI-generated content may be deemed plagiarism or a form of academic misconduct;

WHEREAS, a recent study from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology’s (MIT) Media Lab revealed that students who heavily relied on generative AI tools showed significantly lower brain activity and critical thinking performance, as measured through EEG scans, compared to those who wrote without digital assistance;

WHEREAS, according to the 2020 Functional Literacy, Education, and Mass 
Media Survey by the Philippine Statistics Authority, 8.7 million Filipinos aged 10 to 64 are functionally illiterate, and based on the 2022 World Bank assessment, 90.9% of 10-year-old children in the country could not read and understand a simple text, a condition described as “learning poverty”; 

WHEREAS, in a context of ongoing educational challenges, including poor reading comprehension and functional illiteracy, the unregulated use of AI tools in education may further compromise the development of analytical, reflective, and original thinking among Filipino learners; 

WHEREAS, there is an urgent necessity to establish a national framework on the responsible use of Generative Artificial Intelligence (AI) in education, which shall provide clear, enforceable, and future-responsive guidelines to govern AI-related academic conduct which should include:

1. Definition of what constitutes academic misconduct in relation to AI use; 
2. Establish a clear disclosure and citation protocols for AI-assisted submissions; 
3. Implement an AI literacy training for students, educators, and administrators; 
4. Provide safeguards to ensure AI supports rather than replaces human thinking and learning; 
5. Redesign assessment methods to encourage original thinking and reduce AI dependency;

WHEREAS, international references such as the 2023 UNESCO Guidance on Generative AI in Education and the AI-related educational policies in countries such as Singapore and South Korea may serve as models in crafting a Philippine national policy on the ethical integration of AI in education; 

WHEREAS, In light of the rapid advancement and integration of artificial intelligence in academic activities, it is incumbent upon Congress to act with urgency and foresight by enacting a national policy framework on the responsible use of AI in education, in order to preserve academic integrity, protect cognitive development, and prevent this emerging issue from undermining the gains of ongoing educational reforms in the country; 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, to direct the appropriate House Committee to conduct an inquiry, in aid of legislation, on the responsible and ethical use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in the Philippine Education System, with the end in view of crafting a national policy framework that promotes academic integrity, supports human cognition, and safeguards learning in the age of Generative AI.
