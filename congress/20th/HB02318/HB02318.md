# Introduced by Representatives BELLA VANESSA B. SUANSING and MIKAELA ANGELA B. SUANSING

# AN ACT CRIMINALIZING THE NON-CONSENSUAL USE OF FACE AND LIKENESS OF PERSONS IN CONTENT CREATED THROUGH ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE OR THROUGH ANY OTHER COMPUTER-GENERATED OR TECHNOLOGICAL MEANS, THEREBY DEFINING THE CRIME OF DIGITAL FORGERY IN RELATION TO THE ACTS TO BE CRIMINALIZED, AMENDING FOR THE PURPOSE REPUBLIC ACT NO. 10175, OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE “CYBERCRIME PREVENTION ACT OF 2012”

# EXPLANATORY NOTE

In the pursuit of safeguarding Filipinos from threats posed by digital manipulation on personal privacy and integrity, especially in the advent of the epoch of artificial intelligence, the following bill aims to criminalize the non-consensual use of a person’s face and likeness in content created through artificial intelligence or any other similar computer-generated or technological means. This proposed measure aims to amend for this purpose Republic Act No. 10175, or the Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012, particularly to introduce the crime of digital forgery as a distinct criminal offense.

This proposed measure adopts for the purpose the definition of the crime of “digital forgery” as described in United States Public Law No. 119-12, and augments to this definition to adapt the term to the Philippine context. This definition provides a succinct basis to prosecute acts that were previously ambiguous under existing laws, considering the novel nature of content involving the use of artificial intelligence.

Proposals under this bill seek to provide legal recourse to anyone whose face and likeness was used without their consent, and extends to penalize not only those who generate unauthorized content but also those who publish, distribute, sell, promote or advertise such content, actions which, whether done directly or inadvertently, enable the growth of synthetic media that contribute to the abuse and harm of individuals. Heavier penalties are also prescribed for cases involving malicious intent, particularly when digital forgery is used to defame or damage the honor and dignity of a person. This proposed measure also imposes stiffer sanctions on individuals or entities who receive financial compensation for creating, distributing, promoting, or advertising forged content, underscoring the seriousness of monetizing harm against another person’s identity or reputation especially through new forms of technological advancements.

On the other hand, and in the pursuit of the principles of fairness and impartiality under law, this proposed measure also provides careful boundaries of criminal responsibility and places legal parameters to restrict the scope of punitive action against possible offenders. Under provisions of this proposed measure, criminal intent must first be established on the part of those who share or repost visual content produced or created through digital forgery before offenders may be held liable for penalties under law. Moreover, to ensure that well-intentioned individuals are safeguarded from undue prosecution, persons who committed the act of publishing, distributing, selling, advertising or promoting content created through digital forgery while making a good faith effort to exhaustively verify that the content was not digitally forged will not be held criminally liable under proposed provisions in this bill.

The proposal of this measure, at this point in time, aims not only to establish robust legal protections but also to foster a culture of vigilance and responsibility among the public. The country is at the precipice of sweeping transformations ushered in by artificial intelligence. The current moment necessitates a legal framework ready and prepared to address the pernicious and malicious by-products of this technology as such content threatens to ravage the country’s digital ecosystem. Regulatory mechanisms must evolve, if not faster than, at the rate at which technology evolves. Rapid digitalization has now enabled opportunities for cybercriminals to harm individuals and organizations, instigating new forms of criminality and expanding the range of cybercrimes. The State, with its mandate to protect all Filipinos from all types of attack, must exhaust all powers to fortify existing national frameworks and assure the preservation of personal security, identity, integrity and dignity of all Filipinos.

With these provisions introduced, there must also be a corresponding call for the public to practice digital responsibility, especially when using such a powerful technological tool such as artificial intelligence. Confronted with our new reality, there must be a collective effort on the part of the government and its people to balance the critical right to freedom of expression with the imperative to protect individual rights in the digital realm.

In view of the foregoing, the approval of this bill is earnestly sought.
