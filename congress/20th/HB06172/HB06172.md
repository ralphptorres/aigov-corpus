# EXPLANATORY NOTE

This bill aims to integrate Artificial Intelligence (AI) Education in the basic, secondary, tertiary, and technical-vocational levels to introduce and effectively implement Artificial Intelligence for daily applications of its basic concepts and tools that will enable them and the country to match with worldwide technological standards.

Artificial Intelligence made its way into the society in 1955. However, it was only around 2017 when the term ‘Artificial Intelligence’ was gradually introduced to Filipinos, but not as a systematically integrated technology but as an emerging topic, especially for college students who took an interest and made it into their thesis topics.

The government likewise took an interest with AI when the Department of Trade and Industry (DTI) formally launched the ‘National Artificial Intelligence Roadmap’ in 2021 with the main goal of making the Philippines an “AI Centre of Excellence”. In 2023, the Department of Science and Technology (DOST) reported having invested in various AI related projects to boost the economic status of the country. In fact, in 2024, UNESCO reported that the Philippines is included in the international AI governance spaces.

Nowadays, Artificial Intelligence has now made progress amongst Filipinos, especially to the youth, mostly because of the technological advances made by science and technology in the country. Students have likewise used AI with their projects and assignments which have made their lives easier and efficient. With the many demands of school, the useful help of AI has unloaded them with a lot of burdens and responsibilities. However, the use of AI can still be dangerous especially without any proper understanding of it and its ethical considerations. Therefore, this bill would help diminish any kind of possible damages by integrating AI education in schools’ learning modules to help students understand it and make informed decisions on it.

Therefore, in view of the foregoing, the early approval of this bill is earnestly requested.

# AN ACT INSTITUTIONALIZING ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE (AI) EDUCATION PROGRAM IN ALL EDUCATIONAL LEVELS, AND APPROPRIATING FUNDS THEREFOR

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title. - This Act shall be known as the "Artificial Intelligence (AI) Education In All Levels”

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy. – It is the policy of the State to give priority to education, including science and technology to foster patriotism and nationalism, accelerate social progress, and promote total human liberation through the use of artificial intelligence.

The State shall give priority to research and development, invention, innovation, and their utilization, and to science and technology education, training, and services, especially with the speedy introduction and progress of Artificial Intelligence worldwide.

SECTION. 3. Objectives. – This Act shall have the following objectives:

a. To tailor the curriculum of basic, secondary, tertiary, and technical-vocational education aligned with the standards of worldwide education to ensure that Filipino students match their abilities with the demands of science and technology;   
b. To train educators and administrators to effectively guide and instruct students with Artificial Intelligence;   
c. To foster critical thinking and problem solving skills amongst students in all educational levels with the introduction of basic and advance concepts and tools of Artificial Intelligence;

d. To encourage creative, interactive, and experiential learning environments for students to learn and apply the tools of Artificial Intelligence; and e. To make education accessible to students who are in remote and indigenous areas, as well as to students with disabilities.

SECTION. 4. Integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) Education in the Curriculum of Education – The Department of Education (DepEd), the Commission on Higher Education (CHED), and the Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA), in coordination with the Department of Science and Technology (DOST) and the Department of Information and Communications Technology (DICT), shall create and integrate age-appropriate programs, courses, electives, and other similar policies that aims to gradually introduce Artificial Intelligence and its tools into the basic, secondary, and tertiary education.

These programs, courses, electives, and other similar policies shall be aligned with the grade level of students and shall be updated and elevated each year level:

a. Basic Education – Introduction of the basic concepts of Artificial Intelligence through computational thinking, digital ethics, and problem-solving activities, Provided, That the introduction of AI to students from Grades 1-3 shall be age appropriate as their foundation in understanding AI and its applications to different sectors while teaching them responsible means of using AI technology.   
b. Secondary Education – Application of principles, basic tools, and digital literacy in subjects such as mathematics and science and technology;   
c. Tertiary Education – University and Colleges shall offer courses or degrees specially designed for Artificial Intelligence education, and integrate elective courses for non-AI courses or degrees;   
d. Tech-Voc Education – TESDA shall design competency-based training programs in Artificial Intelligence Technologies, based on the needs of industries.

SECTION. 5. Capacity Building and Training of Educators. - DepEd, CHED and TESDA shall collectively develop AI training and certification programs for all kinds of educators, administrators, and education personnel to ensure effective teaching and learning within their classrooms and effective implementation of AI education.

SECTION. 6. Ethical and Responsible Use of AI Education. – DepEd, CHED, and TESDA shall likewise integrate into the curriculum modules on ethical standards in using Artificial Intelligence, as well data privacy, and human rights to ensure responsible use of its technologies.

SECTION. 7. Research and Development of AI Education . – The government shall allocate funds and incentives to support AI research centers and laboratories within educational institutions.

SECTION. 8. Private Partnership for AI Education. – Deped, CHED, and TESDA shall actively partner with private institutions to ensure the provision, availability and utilization of computers/laptops, software, and internet connection, and other tools necessary for the effective learning of AI Education.

SECTION. 9. Incentives to Educational Institutions. – The government shall incentive and recognize schools who demonstrate exemplary performance in integrating Artificial Intelligence with the following, to be determined by DepEd, CHED, and TESDA:

a. Financial grants   
b. Tax Incentives   
c. Scholarships to students of each school with exemplary performances in AI related subjects or in AI courses   
d. Laboratory Equipment and tools   
e. Technological related equipment and tools

SECTION. 10. Implementing Rules and Regulations. - DepEd, CHED, and TESDA in coordination with the DOST, DICT, and other stakeholders shall formulate the implementing rules and regulations of this Act within ninety (90) days after its approval.

SECTION 11. Appropriations. - The amount necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act shall be included in the General Appropriations Act of the year following its enactment into law and thereafter.

SECTION. 12. Separability Clause. - If any provision of this Act shall be held unconstitutional or invalid, the other provisions not otherwise affected shall remain in full force and effect.

SECTION. 13. Repealing Clause. - All laws, executive orders or issuances or any part thereof, which are inconsistent herewith, are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.

SECTION. 14. Effectivity. - This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation.
