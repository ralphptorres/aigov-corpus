# AN ACTESTABLISHING THE RIGHTS AND WELFARE OF PLATFORM WORKERS,ENSURING FAIR COMPENSATION,SOCIAL PROTECTION, ANDMECHANISMS FOR ACCOUNTABILITY AND COLLECTIVE REPRESENTATION,AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES

# EXPLANATORY NOTE

As the Pulitzer Center aptly putit in its 2025 report: “Algorithmic work, in its current form,risks creating a modern-day labor underclass unless it is balanced with meaningful safeguards and legal rights."

In recent years, the Philippine labor force has seen a sharp rise in the number of individuals engaged in platform-based work-riders,couriers,drivers,and digital freelancers who deliver services through mobile applications such as Grab, Foodpanda, Angkas,and Lalamove. This evolving work landscape has contributed to convenience, mobility,and income generation, especially amid the COVID-19 pandemic. Yet, it has also surfaced significant legal and labor gaps, exposing a growing class of workers to systemic vulnerabilities.

At present, these workers are largely classified as “independent contractors" or "partners," rather than as regular employees.While this classification may provide platforms with operational flexibility,it often deprives the workers of even the most basic labor protections-minimum wage guarantees,access to health benefits, job security,and the right to organize. As found by Fairwork Philippines in its 2023 report, “no major platform operating in the country met all fioe principles offair work," and most riders "ended up earning below the statutory minimum wage once expenses were factored in" (Fairwork Ratings 2023).

The lack of clear legal recognition has allowed some platforms to implement unilateral algorithmic decisions-deactivating accounts，reducing per-task rates,or redistributing bookings-without recourse for the worker. In several cases documented by the Business & Human Rights Resource Centre and GMA News,delivery riders were suspended or permanently deactivated for joining protest actions or voicing concerns about pay cuts. These incidents raise alarming questions about freedom of association and due process.

In response to these recurring abuses, the Philippine Institute for Development Studies has underscored the importance of crafting a “rights-based policy approach" for gig economy workers. Similarly, organized labor groups such as RIDERS-SENTRO have successfully registered as a legitimate union and pushed for sectoral recognition-an important milestone that calls for institutional reinforcement through legislation.

This bill seeks to close these gaps and to establish a legal framework that acknowledges the realities of platform-based labor while protecting the dignity, security, and rights of the individuals engaged in it. It does not seek to prohibit digital work nor limit innovation.Rather, it ensures that innovation does not come at the cost of fairness and human dignity.

In view of te foregoing, the immediate passage of this billis earnestly sought.

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title.- This Act shall be known as the “Platform Workers Rights and Protection Act."

SEC.2.Declaration of Policy. - The State recognizes the right of every worker to decent work, fair compensation, and adequate social protection, regardless of the mode or form of employment. As the labor market evolves with technological advancements, the government acknowledges the rise of the platform economy and commits to ensuring that no worker is left unprotected.This Act seeks to provide a regulatory framework that guarantees minimum labor rights and welfare benefits for individuals performing work mediated through digital platforms, while also supporting innovation and economic flexibility.

# SEC.3.Definition of Terms.- For purposes of this Act, the following terms shall

# mean:

a. Platform Worker refers to any individual who performs Iabor or services through a digital platform, regardless of employment status, including but not limited to delivery personnel，ride-hailing drivers，online freelance workers，virtual assistants, and creative service providers.   
b.Digital Platform refers to any internet-based application or website that mediates and facilitates the matching of demand for and supply of labor or services between a client or consumer and a platform worker.   
c. Algorithmic Management refers to the use of computer algorithms and data analytics to assign tasks， set pricing， evaluate performance，and make employment-related decisions for platform workers.

SEC. 4. Coverage.- This Act shall apply to any person performing remunerated services through digital platforms operating in the Philippines，regardless of employment status or classification.

SEC. 5. Presumption of Employment Relationship. - In cases of dispute， the existence of an employment relationship shall be presumed if the platform exercises control over essential aspects of the work such as pricing， scheduling,customer assignment, or performance evaluation. Platforms may rebut this presumption by proving that the worker is genuinely independent under criteria to be established by the Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE).

# SEC.6.Rights and Entitlements of Platform Workers.- All platform workers shall be entitled to the following rights and benefits:

a.Fair and Transparent Compensation - Platform workers shall be entitled to compensation not less than the prevailing regional minimum wage per hour, prorated accordingly for piece-rate or per-task arrangements, exclusive of tips or gratuities voluntarily given by clients. All deductions, fees, commissions, or charges applicable to the transaction shall be fully disclosed by the platform prior to the worker's acceptance of the assignment.

b.Social Protection and Welfare Benefits- Platform-based digital labor shall not be excluded from coverage under mandatory social protection programs. Accordingly，platforms shall enroll their dependent contractors in the Social Security System (SSS), Philippine Health Insurance Corporation (PhilHealth), and the Home Development Mutual Fund (Pag-IBIG)，with the contribution responsibilities shared proportionately between the platform and the worker,as may be prescribed by regulation. Furthermore, platforms shallsecure mandatory accidental death and medical insurance coverage for all active workers during the performance of their duties.

c.Right to Information and Algorithmic Transparency- Platform workers shall be fully informed of their legal rights, modes of remuneration, rating systems, grounds for deactivation or suspension, and any algorithmic or operational changes that may materially affect their terms of engagement or income potential. All terms of service shall be drafted in clear and plain language,and made readily accessible through mobile and electronic devices.

d.Right to Safe and Healthy Working Conditions-Platforms shall ensure that workers are provided with the necessary personal protective equipment，weatherappropriate clothing, and safety tools commensurate to the nature of the work performed.In transportation-related services, platforms shall provide adequate training on road safety, accident protocols, and proper health hygiene standards, and ensure that assistance is available in case of emergencies.

e. Right to Organize and Collective Bargaining - Platform workers shall have the right to organize themselves, join labor organizations，cooperatives,or workers' associations,and engage in collective bargaining or mutual aid activities. The exercise of this right shall not be a basis for retaliation,reduction of task assignments, or algorithmic demotion.

f.Protection Against Unjust Deactivation - No platform worker shall be suspended, penalized, de-prioritized,or permanently deactivated without observance of due process. This includes the right to prior notice, written explanation of the grounds for such action, and an opportunity to be heard and to appeal the decision before an impartial body or mechanism instituted by the platform.

g.Protection from Fraud and Abuse - Platform workers shall be protected against fraud,abuse, and deceptive practices committed by users or platform clients. Where a worker incurs out-of-pocket expenses in the performance of a task-such as advance payment for goods, services,or transportation-only for the booking to be discovered as fraudulent, the platform shall bear the responsibility of refunding or reimbursing the affected worker within a reasonable period. The reimbursement process shall be simple, accessible,and not require burdensome documentation. Platforms shall Iikewise establish a swift grievance redress mechanism specifically dedicated to incidents involving fraudulent transactions.

h. Non-Discrimination - All platform workers shall be treated equally and without discrimination based on gender，age，disability，ethnicity，religion， sexual orientation,political affiliation,or membership in any legitimate workers' organization. The principle of equal opportunity and fair treatment shall be upheld in all aspects of engagement, including access to task assignments, client ratings, and dispute resolution.
