# HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 498

Introduced by Representative RAYMOND ADRIAN SALCEDA

# RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING THE TECHNICAL EDUCATION AND SKILLS DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY(TESDA) AS A KEY INSTITUTION FOR PREPARING THE FILIPINO WORKFORCE FOR ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, SUPPORTING A NATIONAL AGENDA FOR AI READINESS,AND URGING THE PRESIDENT TO FORMULATE AND ANNOUNCE A NATIONAL ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE POLICY FOR GOVERNMENT

WHEREAS, artificial intelligence is rapidly transforming production, services, public administration，and labor markets around the world, creating opportunities for higher productivity and new forms of work,while also posing risks of job displacement and skills obsolescence for workers and enterprises that are unprepared;

WHEREAS,a significant proportion of Filipino workers will require retraining, reskilling, and upskilling in the coming years in order to remain competitive in an economy increasingly shaped by artificial intelligence and automation;

WHEREAS, the Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA) serves as the national authority for technical vocational education and training,with the mandate to provide accessible, industry-relevant, and responsive workforce development programs across the country;

WHEREAS, TESDA has begun integrating artificial intelligence into its competency standards, curricula, and training programs, including the development of micro-credential offerings on generative artificial intelligence that emphasize responsible use， ethical considerations, and data privacy;

WHEREAS, TESDA, through the TESDA Online Program and through partnerships with public and private institutions, has expanded access to artificial intelligence and emerging technology courses, including AI fundamentals, machine learning essentials,and related digital workforce skills;

WHEREAS, TESDA has supported local and regional initiatives on AI readiness, including collaborative programs with local governments, academic institutions, and development actors to prepare workers,learners, and micro,small, and medium enterprises for an AI-enabled economy;

WHEREAS, TESDA's expanding work on artificial intelligence demonstrates its crucial role as the frontline government institution capable of providing scaled, practical,and industry-responsive AI training and capacity building for Filipino workers,learners,and public servants;

WHEREAS, there is an urgent need for a clear,unified,and forward-looking national artificial intelligence policy for the public sector,which shall guide ethical use of AI in government, ensure capacity building for civil servants, promote responsible deployment of AI systems, and support whole-of-government coordination on AI readiness;

NOW,THEREFORE,BE IT RESOLVED,AS IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize the Technical Education and Skills Development Authority as a key institution for preparing the Filipino workforce for artificial intelligence and express its strong support for the expansion, deepening，and strengthening of TESDA's programs on AI readiness, digital skills, and emerging technologies.

RESOLVED, FURTHER，That the House of Representatives urge the national government to provide adequate budgetary support to TESDA for the development of competency standards, training programs,assessment systems,and partnerships related to artificial intelligence and advanced digital skills,including regional and local AI readiness initiatives.

RESOLVED,FURTHER,That the House of Representatives respectfully urge the President of the Republic of the Philippines to formulate and announce a national artificial intelligence policy for government, which shall provide strategic direction on AI readiness for the public sector, promote ethical and responsible use of AI,and identify TESDA and other concerned agencies as key implementing partners for workforce training and capacity building.

RESOLVED,FINALLY, That copies of this Resolution be furnished to the Office of the President, the Technical Education and Skills Development Authority,the Department of Labor and Employment, the Department of Information and Communications Technology, the Department of Economy,Planning,and Development, and other concerned agencies for their information and appropriate action.

Adopted,

RAYMOND ADRIAN SALCEDA
